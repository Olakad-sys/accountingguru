Financial Statements

There are three main reports: Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement.

Income Statement

Shows revenues and expenses for a period → calculates profit or loss.

Example: Sales R10,000 - Expenses R6,000 = Net profit R4,000.

Balance Sheet

Shows what the business owns (assets) and owes (liabilities) at a point in time. Assets = Liabilities + Equity.

Cash Flow Statement

Shows how cash moved in and out (operating, investing, financing).

Try it — Exercise

Q: If a company has assets R50,000 and liabilities R30,000, what is equity?

Answer

Equity = Assets - Liabilities = R20,000.
