Introduction to Accounting

Accounting is the process of recording, organizing, and summarizing money-related events so people can make good decisions. Businesses use accounting to know if they made profit, how much they owe, and how much they own.

Why it matters (simple):

  • Owners check profit or loss.
  • Banks decide to lend based on financial info.
  • Managers plan budgets and control costs.

Example

If you sell stationery for R100 cash, you record that R100 came in and revenue increased by R100.

Try it — Exercise

Q: If you buy a laptop for R8,000 in cash for the business, what happened?

Answer

You received an asset (Laptop +R8,000) and paid out cash (Cash -R8,000). Total equity doesn't change.
