Assets, Liabilities & Equity

Assets = things the business owns (cash, inventory, equipment). Liabilities = what it owes (loans, payables). Equity = owner's claim (capital + retained earnings).

Example

Owner invests R20,000 cash → Assets (Cash) +R20,000 and Equity (Capital) +R20,000.

Try it — Exercise

Q: Business borrows R5,000 from the bank. Write the simple effect on BS.

Answer

Assets (Cash) +R5,000 and Liabilities (Bank Loan) +R5,000.
